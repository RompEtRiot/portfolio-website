PlutoPay

Hidden transaction fees and marked up exchange rates

These extra-costs are felt by those travelling,boarding, and shopping abroad — in person and online. PlutoPay is a thesis project that answers the call for a cost-conscious and secure way to pay for personal and group expenses, outside of one’s home or host country.

  • Role —Lead user experience designer
  • Responsibility —Business strategy, information architecture, user interviews, affinity mapping, usability testing, interface design
  • Team —Robert Surrency, Mentor and Jessica Parra-Fitch, Advisor
  • Duration —11 months

Challenge

How will PlutoPay distinguish itself from a litany of payment apps already available? This was the first question I asked myself, and set about solving for. To inform this question I conducted a SWOT analysis of PlutoPay’s closest competitors, and learned:

  • Roadblocks —The primary roadblocks faced by payment apps are zero fee transparency, partial transaction tracking, and choosing between slow service vs high fees.
  • Competition —Apps that allow users to transfer and spend currency domestically and internationally — especially those who provide competitive exchange rates. Included but not limited to Venmo, PayPal, and Wise.

Opportunity abound

PlutoPay will facilitate group transactions in addition to individual transactions both domestically and internationally, at mid-market and transparent rates. Positioning itself as both transparent and specialized, serves a dual purpose: (1) Distinguishes PlutoPay from all-purpose competitors, and (2) allows PlutoPay to operate in the Canadian market — as the app is not seen as competing directly with Canada’s banking institution.

Audience

The app's biggest draw is that it facilitates not only individual and domestic transactions, but group and international transactions as well. There are many reasons why a user would seek an app with this type of dual-purpose, as represented by the personae. The personae were informed by user interviews and supplementary research. Findings not only supported the decision to specialize in group and domestic transactions but helped to identify the key demographic: millennial and Gen-Z.

Group Traveller

Core motivation is to share expenses while travelling internationally with their friends.

Solo Traveller

Core motivation is to set personal spending limits and alerts to manage their spending.

International Student

Core motivation is transparency concerning fee summaries and transaction journeys..

Online Shopper

Core motivation is is expense tracking and saving.

Millennials and Gen-Z are most likely to own a phone, travel outside of their own country, and shop internationally.

  • 91% of shoppers shop online using their smartphone.
  • 95% of Gen-Z own a smartphone and use it for 10+ hours per day.
  • 57% of online shoppers shop internationally.

When interviewing this audience interviewees expressed that they:

  • Seek payment apps with predictable, traceable, and accurate, transaction journeys, to lessen their anxieties.
  • Will convert to a payment app if it solves a situational need that can not be solved with traditional means.

Creation

Affinity Mapping

While the motivations of personae informed PlutoPay’s mobile-first approach, user interviews were used to prioritize features. After grouping qualitative information into categories (affinity mapping), features were sorted based on required, desirable, predicted and unpredicted.

Required Features Desirable Features
Track and request transfers Enable reminders and set due dates
Choose from paid and unpaid transfer methods Rate users based on total peer to peer transfers paid on time
Succinct fee summaries Visually track transfer journey
Create multiple virtual cards Sign-out of all active devices using global logout
Create group cards Search through and filter transaction history
Temporarily freeze a card, cards, or an account Rush transactions via a small fee

User Flows

Card Sort

After completing the initial site-map of the app a card sort was conducted, in which we learned and applied the following:

  • Expense history:Access card specific expense histories.
  • Global expense:Integrate expense features throughout the app, opposed to isolated to a single page.
  • Foreign vs home transfers:Pay or receive in a foreign or home currency, during the transfer process.
  • Freeze card:Freeze a card via profile and within settings.
  • Separate flows:Separate features related to cards and currency.

Testing

Usability Test Insights

Completion of high fidelity wireframes were followed by both a usability test and a preference test. This informed where and how to refine the wireframes, as well as time allocation.

Goal

  1. Observing the speed and accuracy of task completion and;
  2. Gathering qualitative information on how participants understood core features and their relation to each-other.

Objectives

  • Do participants understand that the app facilitates both domestic and foreign transfers?
  • Do participants understand that currencies and cards held operate independently from one another?
  • Do participants understand that currencies refer to monies held and transfers are the sending of held monies?

Currency use

Problem:Unclear that currencies are not tethered to individual cards.

Solution:

(a) Add the ability for users to limit the currencies an card can draw from, with the inferred default being draw from any available currency.

Transfer method

Problem: Unclear that transfers can draw from users currency portfolio.

Solution:

(b) Notify users if they have held currencies available that they can draw from for the transfer.

(c) Allow users to transfer from held currencies, if the transfer currency and held currency match.

Trust Rating

Problem: Same symbol is used to denote trust rating and primary card.

Solution:

(d) Change trust ratings star to coloured-dot system. In which red means warning, yellow means caution, and green means trustworthy.

Execution

Quick dash

The dashboard provides easy access to a person’s primary card and recent activity. Allowing them to make purchases or transfer on dashboard load.

  • Access primary card
  • Freeze primary card
  • Quick buy or transfer
  • View recent activity

Pay, your way

Users can select from multiple transfer methods with varying fees. These fees allow PlutoPay to expand its suite of holdable currencies that would otherwise be too expensive to adopt. Perfect for travel.

  • Multiple transfer speeds
  • Low-cost options
  • Fee transparency
  • Multiple methods

Clear cut costs

Fees are highlighted during the course of all PlutoPay transactions. Ensuring exchange and transfer fees do not catch users off-guard.

  • Fee estimation
  • Archive transactions

What I learned

The information density of the transfer and currency flows posed an interesting challenge. Engineering ways of living up to PlutoPays dedication to transparency, while crafting a straightforward user experience and maintaining a clean interface, was rewarding.

What I would change

I would expand on how expenses are tracked and categorized. Initially a budgeting system was planned, however user interviews revealed that traditional budgeting methods did not resonate with users. I’d like to explore these attitudes further using targeted surveys and a larger pool of interviewees.

Next steps

The next step would be optimizing PlutoPay for the web. PlutoPay’s audience are primarily mobile-first and on-the-go. Which informed the mobile-first approach to the project, and the prioritization of a mobile prototype for the purpose of testing. However, the student and shopper personae would benefit from the implementation of a web-app.
